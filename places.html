Helsinki

It is the capital and main entry point to Finland, a modern and peaceful city where neoclassical architecture blends with contemporary Nordic design, the sea is always nearby, and there is a strong culture of cafes, markets, and saunas that perfectly reflect the Finnish lifestyle.

Suomenlinna

It is a maritime fortress located on several islands opposite Helsinki and declared a World Heritage Site, famous for its historical importance, its walls, tunnels and cannons, and for being an ideal place to walk along the sea and understand the country's military past.

Laponia (Rovaniemi)

It represents the most extreme and symbolic Finland, located in the Arctic Circle, with snowy landscapes, endless forests and a strong connection with Sami culture, in addition to being known worldwide as the "home of Santa Claus".

La región de los lagos (Saimaa)

It is the natural heart of Finland, with thousands of lakes surrounded by forests, wooden cabins and traditional saunas, an environment that symbolizes calm, connection with nature and the most authentic way of life in the country.

Auroras boreales (Inari)

Finnish Lapland, located far north of the Arctic Circle with minimal light pollution, is surrounded by pristine nature, frozen lakes like Lake Inari, and exceptionally clear skies, making the Northern Lights frequently visible during the winter and the most reliable and spectacular spot in the country to observe this natural phenomenon.
