Despite the recovery, several "headwinds" remain a priority for the Finnish government:

  1. Public Deficit: Finland’s debt-to-GDP ratio is significantly higher than its Nordic neighbors (like Sweden or Denmark), leading to continued pressure for fiscal "tightening" or budget cuts.

  2. Labor Shortages: While unemployment is high in some sectors, there is a chronic shortage of skilled workers in healthcare and technology.

  3. Geopolitical Uncertainty: Being on the border of Russia means trade remains redirected toward Western allies, and the economy is sensitive to broader European security and trade tensions.
